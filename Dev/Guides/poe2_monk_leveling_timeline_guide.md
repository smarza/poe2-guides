## Executive Summary

This run levels a **Monk** through the campaign using three main phases:

1. **Early Act 1: Power Charge burst setup**  
   Use basic Monk attacks, generate **Power Charges**, then spend them for big burst. Add **Frozen Locus** defensively and later **Frost Bomb** for exposure.

2. **Late Act 1 to Act 2: Lightning conversion / Tempest Bell phase**  
   Add **Tempest Bell**, **Herald of Thunder**, **chance to shock**, and a cheap talisman/interaction that grants **extra lightning damage** after using Power Charges. Damage becomes much stronger, but the rotation has many buttons.

3. **Act 2 / Act 3 onward: Siphoning Strike shock/crit build**  
   Around **01:50:12**, the build changes into the real version: **shock enemies, then consume/remove shock with Siphoning Strike** for huge damage. The build then scales through **critical hit chance**, **extra elemental damage**, **Power Charges**, and weapon upgrades.

The biggest practical lessons are:

- Check vendors often for **quarterstaff upgrades**.
- Prioritize **physical damage**, **elemental damage**, **critical hit chance/damage**, **life**, and enough defenses.
- The build is very weapon-sensitive early, but once the Siphoning Strike setup comes online, it performs well even with a not-perfect quarterstaff.
- Do not overcomplicate the rotation. The creator experiments a lot, but the final practical loop becomes much simpler.

## Core Build Concept

### Early concept

You start weak and squishy, so the early gameplay is:

1. Pull or isolate enemies.
2. Generate **Power Charges**.
3. Use your charge-spending burst to delete packs or rares.
4. Use **Frozen Locus** to control dangerous melee packs.
5. Add **Frost Bomb** to expose enemies and improve damage.

### Midgame concept

The build adds more buttons:

- **Tempest Bell** for big single-target and pack damage.
- **Frost Bomb** for exposure.
- **Herald of Thunder** for clear speed.
- Lightning damage scaling from shock/Power Charge interactions.
- A cheap talisman/offhand setup that grants extra lightning damage after using Power Charges.

This is powerful but clunky. Do not get stuck trying to perfect every combo. It is a temporary bridge to the final version.

### Final practical concept

The real build starts around **01:50:12**:

1. Shock enemy.
2. Enemy stands on shocked ground or remains shocked through repeated hits.
3. Use **Siphoning Strike** to consume/remove shock and deal huge damage.
4. Scale with critical hit chance, critical damage, elemental damage, Power Charges, and exposure.

The creator’s words imply the build becomes much simpler here: Tempest Bell and the talisman are no longer mandatory, though they can still be used if you prefer the extra mechanics.

## Passive Tree / Ascendancy Priorities

The transcript does not provide a full passive tree, but the priorities are clear.

### Early passive priorities

- Damage first, especially **physical/attack damage**.
- Path toward **chance to shock** around **00:25:07**.
- Early stable damage is acceptable, but the creator intentionally moves toward shock/crit instead.

### Weapon set idea

The creator experiments with weapon set separation:

- **Weapon set 1:** skill speed / attack speed / smoother clear.
- **Weapon set 2:** critical strike / boss burst.

However, he also notes a conflict with the talisman/wyvern interaction. The practical final version uses shared/weapon set mechanics after Siphoning Strike comes online.

### Ascendancy

At **01:25:30**, choose **Invoker**.

The selected direction is:

- Shock magnitude.
- 10% damage as extra lightning damage.
- Chance for shocking enemies to create shocked ground.

This is central to why Siphoning Strike becomes spammable and powerful: enemies remain shocked or are repeatedly re-shocked via shocked ground.

### Later passive priorities

- Critical hit chance.
- Critical hit chance while you have Power Charges.
- Critical hit chance against enemies with exposure.
- Potential stun build-up investment is mentioned later as interesting, but not required.
- Some defensive nodes are needed because the creator repeatedly notes that Monk is fragile.

## Gear Rules

### General item priorities

For armor and accessories:

- Life.
- Resistances when needed.
- Armor/evasion or energy shield if useful.
- Spirit if it enables important buffs.
- Do not ignore defense; most deaths/dangerous moments come from being too squishy.

For weapons:

- Quarterstaff upgrades are the most important power spikes.
- Look for **increased physical damage**.
- Later, also value **critical damage**, **critical hit chance**, and **increased elemental damage**.
- Add sockets/runes when the base is good enough.
- Use Blacksmith’s Whetstones / quality upgrades on strong bases.

### Vendor strategy

From around **00:44:23**, start checking town vendors regularly, especially after leveling. The creator says vendor upgrades can almost double DPS.

In Act 2, this becomes even more important:

- Sell rare/yellow items to build currency.
- Buy one or two good quarterstaff bases.
- Do not waste currency on bad suffixes.
- Prioritize prefixes, especially damage prefixes.

## Rotation by Phase

### Phase 1: First minutes / early Act 1

Use this until Tempest Bell and lightning scaling come online.

```text
Isolate enemies → generate Power Charges → spend/burst → repeat
```

Add Frozen Locus when surrounded:

```text
Place Frozen Locus → reposition → attack isolated enemies → generate charges
```

### Phase 2: Frost Bomb + Tempest Bell transition

Use this against rares and bosses:

```text
Frost Bomb / exposure → Tempest Bell → hit bell and enemy → spend Power Charges → repeat
```

For packs:

```text
Frozen Locus → attack → generate charges → Falling Thunder / main burst
```

### Phase 3: Lightning/talisman Power Charge burst

After the lightning damage interaction is active:

```text
Generate Power Charges → use charge spender / Rent-style attack → gain extra lightning damage → burst enemies
```

The creator emphasizes that this starts feeling complicated. Treat it as a temporary high-damage bridge.

### Phase 4: Final Siphoning Strike setup

This is the main build.

```text
Shock enemy → Siphoning Strike → shock again / stand on shocked ground → Siphoning Strike again
```

For bosses:

```text
Apply shock → Sniper Mark / exposure / Infernal Cry if available → Siphoning Strike crit burst → repeat
```

When fully buffed, the creator repeatedly shows near one-shots or huge boss health deletion.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Ignoring weapon upgrades**  
   The run’s biggest power spikes come from quarterstaff upgrades. Vendor check often.

2. **Trying to play full combo all the time**  
   The creator repeatedly says long combos are awkward because bosses move, phase, or reset stun/status windows. Use situational combos, not overbuilt combos.

3. **Staying too squishy**  
   Damage is huge, but the character is fragile. Wind Dancer/defensive gear/life matter.

4. **Upgrading a future weapon too early**
