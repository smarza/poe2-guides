Core route identity

The run is not “Ice Shot from level 1.” It is a staged bow campaign route:

Act 1 → early Act 2: Lightning Arrow + Lightning RodAct 2 bossing: Snipe + Frost BombAct 3 onward: Ice Shot clear + Snipe bossesMaps: Ice Shot clear, Snipe burst, Frost Bomb when usable

What makes or breaks the time

The build is strong, but the run depends on four recurring constraints: attribute planning, the chill-magnitude quiver, Frost Bomb staying usable, and avoiding excessive gear inspection.

Most important correction: do not copy the time losses. Copy the route, not every detour.

Primary objective

Rush the early campaign without full-clearing. Your only real objectives are to unlock the early bow package, get Frost Bomb, reach the minimum level breakpoints, and avoid a catastrophic weapon start.

Do this

  • Start Lightning Arrow and pick Lightning Rod immediately when available.
  • Pick the level 1 uncut gem in the abandoned camp and take Frost Bomb.
  • Use Frost Bomb on early bosses: cast it, count roughly 1–5, then recast as the window expires.
  • Take Elemental Armament as the first support power spike.
  • Kill only enough monsters to reach the next requirement; do not full-clear low-value zones.

Early gear priorities

PriorityWhat to look for
1Any usable bow: short bow or flat elemental damage roll.
2Movement speed boots: ID boots early; MS saves minutes over the run.
3Transmute/Augment orbs: enough to make the bow usable.
4Flat fire/lightning/cold damage: any flat damage carries early.
5Basic quiver: damage or accuracy; the god-tier quiver comes later.

Failure point to avoid

The transcript loses several minutes because no good bow drops. The correct play is not endless reset-fishing: buy or craft something mediocre-but-functional, then keep moving. Act 1 can still be recovered even if it ends around 52–55 minutes.

Transition from the Act 1 Lightning Arrow/Lightning Rod setup into Snipe bossing. Act 2 is where the build starts becoming powerful, but also where attribute pressure begins.

Skill transition

  • Keep clearing with Lightning Arrow until the Snipe package is available.
  • At level 19, take Escape Shot, Barrage, and the pieces needed for Snipe.
  • Test Snipe + Hourglass + Window of Opportunity on bosses.
  • Once Snipe starts deleting bosses, stop relying on Lightning Rod.

Bossing logic

The Act 2 bossing package is about creating a freeze/chill damage window, then releasing a fully charged Snipe into that window. Failed one-shots in the run are usually caused by bad Frost Bomb timing, the boss moving/phasing, or releasing Snipe outside the damage window.

The quiver spike

The chill-magnitude damage quiver/mod found around 01:37:01 is one of the biggest reasons the run succeeds. It makes chilled enemies take increased damage based on chill magnitude. Without that quiver or a comparable substitute, expect slower boss kills.

Balbala and ascendancy

Balbala is done even though Lightning Arrow feels awkward against moving bosses. After the quiver spike, the player chooses to ascend because the build has enough damage and control to make the trial safer.

Herald of Ice rule

Herald of Ice is useful, but the transcript spends time looking for the spirit gem and does not get clean value from the detour. Take it if it naturally appears; otherwise keep progressing. Ice Shot already clears well at this stage.

Act 3 route goals

  • Take Ice Shot at level 31.
  • Keep Snipe for bosses; do not try to make Ice Shot do every boss job alone.
  • Grab easy value like the free rare belt, but avoid side detours after the objective is done.
  • Save correct gem levels for Frost Bomb; do not accidentally create a stat/level mismatch.
  • Prepare for the level 41 stat wall before the Act 3 final boss.

Level 41 stat wall

Around level 41, the player upgrades Frost Bomb and Snipe to level 11, then temporarily breaks the setup because of missing Dex/Int. This is the first major “build knowledge” check: do not enter the boss arena with Snipe or Frost Bomb disabled.

Primary objective

Stabilize the build and craft one strong bow that carries into early mapping. Act 4 is not mechanically complex for this build; the main danger is wasting time checking too much gear.

Weapon-set passive setup

Set passives so weapon set 1 supports Dex / bow skills / Ice Shot / Snipe, while weapon set 2 supports Int / Frost Bomb. This prevents the build from breaking every time you upgrade gems.

Major bow upgrade

At around 04:18:30, the creator quality/crafts a bow and says it can carry into at least Tier 10 mapping. This is the practical benchmark: once you have a bow in that range, stop shopping every vendor for marginal replacements.

Act 4 gameplay

  • Ice Shot clears packs and keeps the run fast.
  • Snipe remains the boss burst skill.
  • Use Frost Bomb only when it is actually usable and worth the setup time.
  • Fix fire/cold/lightning resistance as you go; do not enter mapping with completely broken defenses.

Correct decision pattern

The run does not wait for the perfect charge configuration. It uses Wind Dancer when the charge setup is not ready. That is the right mindset for a timed run: use the best functional setup now, not the theoretically best setup later.

Campaign finish prep

Primary objective

Push from first maps to a safe Tier 15 completion. The bottleneck shifts from raw damage to gem level, character level, waystone sustain, resistances, accuracy, and safe map selection.

Early mapping rule

Do not jump tiers faster than your gems and character level can support. Around 06:46:54, the creator realizes he has pushed too high and needs lower-tier gems. If Frost Bomb or Snipe requirements break, farm lower maps instead of brute-forcing.

Mapping priorities

PriorityReason
Character level / gem levelFrost Bomb and other skills repeatedly become unusable or ineffective.
Waystone sustainYou need enough map pool to continue pushing tiers.
ResistancesYellow/red maps punish bad resists quickly.
AccuracyMissing Snipe/Ice Shot wastes boss windows.
Safe modifiersFirst T15 should be completion-focused, not ego-focused.

Tier pushing plan

  • T1–T4: build waystone pool and patch gems.
  • Level 64 target: make Frost Bomb functional again if possible.
  • Yellow maps: fix resists before pushing dangerous mods.
  • T9: continue sustain; do not force bad bosses.
  • T14: area-level problems can make Frost Bomb weak/useless again.
  • T15: run a safe blue map first and avoid bad modifiers.

Mods to avoid for first T15

  • Dual bosses or boss modifiers that make the Snipe window unreliable.
  • Heavy elemental resistance mods when your damage depends on chill/freeze scaling.
  • Dangerous extra damage combinations.
  • Bad layouts if you have alternatives.
  • Anything that makes the first clear risky when a blue T15 is available.
